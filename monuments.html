Hortus Botanicus

The Hortus Botanicus in Leiden is one of the oldest botanical gardens in Europe, established in 1590. Located near the University of Leiden, it spans over 1.5 hectares and features an impressive collection of plants from around the world. The garden is divided into various sections, including tropical greenhouses, a medicinal herb garden, and themed plant displays that showcase biodiversity. Visitors can explore winding paths lined with historic trees, beautiful flower beds, and serene ponds, making it a perfect spot for relaxation and education. The Hortus Botanicus also hosts exhibitions, workshops, and events, contributing to its role as a center for botanical research and public engagement. With its rich history and diverse plant life, the garden is a gem in Leiden, attracting nature enthusiasts, scholars, and casual visitors alike.

Burcht van Leiden

The Burcht van Leiden is a historic fortress situated on a hilltop in the city of Leiden, Netherlands. Originally constructed in the 11th century, it served as a defensive structure and is one of the few remaining castles in the region. The site features a circular earthwork and a stone wall, offering stunning panoramic views of the city and its canals. The Burcht is surrounded by a beautifully landscaped park, making it a popular spot for both locals and tourists to relax and enjoy the scenery. The fortress has a rich history, including its role during the Eighty Years' War when it was key to the city’s defense. Today, visitors can explore the site, learn about its historical significance, and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere, making it a must-visit landmark in Leiden.

Leiden City Hall

Leiden City Hall (Stadhuis van Leiden) is a grand Renaissance-style building that serves as the administrative heart of the city. Originally built in the 14th century, it has been rebuilt several times, with its most iconic façade dating from 1597. The building was heavily damaged by a fire in 1929, but the front was carefully restored to its former glory, while the rest of the structure was modernized. The city hall’s intricate stonework, decorative sculptures, and towering clock face reflect its historical importance. Located on the Breestraat, it has witnessed countless events, from political decisions to cultural celebrations. The interior hosts official ceremonies, council meetings, and public events, preserving its status as both a functional civic center and an important historical landmark in Leiden.
